Interior Design

Multi-Cultural Learning Center

This space was designed as my capstone project. Based upon research of second-language acquisition, the center focuses on not only teaching langauge and culture in tandem but also unites a largely immigrant community in South Philadelphia.

The lot, selected for its corner visibility, is close to several churches and a retail district. In addition, the neighborhood represents a huge diversity ranging from Vietnam to Ethiopia, as well as the original population of Italians who had settled there years before. The first floor program includes a huge courtyard accessible to the public, a cafe, and administrative spaces for community services such as immigration law, interpretation, and dispute mediation. The second and third floors comprise the educational portion of the program: flexible spaces for classrooms, casual and formal meetings, and cultural spaces such as dance studios and lecture halls. The modern, clean, and "nation"less design encourages cooperation and creativity, as well as providing plenty of chances to interact with others.

Start-up HQ Offices

An active start-up marketing an exercise plan that gamifies working out requires an equally playful and active work space. Designed with open planning to ensure employees move frequently and features such as short-term project group offices, an in house gym and game testing site, the HQ remains both cost efficent and aesthetically pleasing.
